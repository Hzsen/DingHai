# High Volatility Regime Notes

High-volatility regimes in the sample data are periods where realized volatility is elevated and drawdowns occur more frequently. Momentum has weak high-volatility performance in the regime_performance table, while the low-volatility and sector-rotation strategies are more resilient.

## Observed Pattern

During high-volatility regimes, momentum_60d has a Sharpe of -0.22 and annual return of -4.1%. Volatility_20d has a Sharpe of 0.61, and sector_relative_strength has a Sharpe of 0.67.

## Risk Notes

High volatility can reduce the reliability of trend continuation signals. It can also increase turnover, transaction costs, and factor crowding risk.
