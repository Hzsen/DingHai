# March 2020 Momentum Underperformance

Momentum underperformed sharply in March 2020 as the COVID shock created a high-volatility drawdown regime. The sample backtest records show momentum_long_short returned -18.2% for March 2020 with a Sharpe of -1.35 and a max drawdown of -21.8%.

## Main Drivers

Prior winners in technology were sold to raise cash, while previously weak defensive and value exposures bounced intermittently during policy-response rallies. This caused a short-horizon momentum reversal.

Crowding also mattered. The anomaly log flags momentum_reversal events for AAPL and MSFT, noting that high prior winners sold off as volatility spiked and investors reduced gross exposure.

## Interpretation

The evidence does not prove a universal causal law. It shows that in this local sample, the momentum strategy was vulnerable to volatility spikes, liquidity stress, and rapid factor rotation during March 2020.
